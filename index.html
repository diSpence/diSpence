Associative Travel Location Acquisition Service

User Manual

With the UN announcement for the first Martian City and the dawn of interplanetary exploration, Conexus has recognized the importance of tracking your precise location. The A.T.L.A.S. device brings this to the new frontier's daring pathfinders.

This manual's contents can be navigated via the top navigation bar.

The Map Display

The screen will display information about visited and surrounding areas:

Legend

Purple represents the Purity Nexus. Undiscovered (grey) hexes are considered "Blind", and moving into them has certain consequences. Red borders indicate the distance from the Purity Nexus or "Tier".

Dark-Gray hexes are "Fissured", require double the travel time and give the party "Near-Sighted", which gives a penalty to discovery checks. Green tiles offer methods of travelling across fissures other than using ziplines, often involving a quest or encounter and may or may not permit the party to turn back.

Setpiece Locations, generally containing quest objectives that progress the story, can be found in white hexes along with opportunities for unique rewards.

Voidspawn hives dot the landscape. Fight off and purify the hordes, and create a temporary ward of purity, enabling the creation of a base camp. The ward disappears upon the creation of the next base-camp, allowing the Voidspawn to return.

Waypoints are rare and scattered around the map. Finding one will reveal a vast swath of the surrounding area.

Base-Camps

The following actions may be taken once per camp:

  • Search for loot (Requires a roll of 16 or higher either Investigation or Perception).

  • Long Rest.

  • Wait out an Ashstorm, consuming 1 day of rations.

  • Recall to and back from the Purity Nexus a single time.

Along with one of the following two actions:

  • Synthesize nearby organic matter into Rations (Make Survival check. Every 5 gives a Ration).

  • Synthesize nearby organic matter into Medicine (Make Medicine check. Every 5 gives a Health Potion of appropriate strength).

Resources

  • TIME >> Each hex requires 12 hours travel (doubled in fissures).

  • RATIONS >> Each 1 day of travel requires 1 unit of rations. Return to last base camp if rations run out.

  • ZIPLINES >> Good for one entrance to or exit from a fissured tile. Persists Indefinitely.

  • BATTERIES/OIL >> Required to remove Near-sighted in a fissure. Use 1 per tile.
